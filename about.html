How it started

A kitchen in Surulere.
A big, stubborn idea.

It started the way most real things do — not with a business plan, but with a feeling. Founder Tolu Adeyemi had spent years watching products she loved slowly compromise on quality once they got bigger. The ingredients would change. The care would go missing. The thing that made it special would vanish quietly, as if no one would notice.

She noticed. And she was tired of it.

In 2019, working out of her mother's kitchen in Surulere, Tolu began making products the way she believed they should be made — small batches, real ingredients, no shortcuts. She gave them to neighbours, to friends, to anyone willing to give honest feedback. The response was always the same: "Where can I buy more of this?"

Growing up

From Surulere
to the whole city.

Word spread the way it only does when something is genuinely good. By 2021, Mobilin had outgrown the kitchen and moved into a small production space in Yaba. The team grew — carefully, deliberately. Every new person hired had to believe in the same thing: that doing it right was non-negotiable, even when it cost more.

Today, Mobilin ships across Lagos and beyond. The city that shaped us is still at the centre of everything we do — our suppliers, our community, our people. Lagos isn't just where we're based. It's who we are.

The kitchen in Surulere is gone. The stubbornness isn't.

Our Mission

To prove that quality and accessibility aren't opposites.

We believe great products shouldn't be reserved for a few. Our mission is to make exceptional quality available to everyone — without ever compromising on what makes our products worth buying in the first place.

Our Vision

A Lagos brand recognised globally for uncompromising craft.

We want Mobilin to be the name people think of when they think of quality from Nigeria. Not just another local brand that made it big — but proof of what's possible when you refuse to settle.
